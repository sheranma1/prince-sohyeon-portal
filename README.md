# Prince Sohyeon Portal

A comprehensive English-language historical portal dedicated to Prince Sohyeon (昭顯世子, 1612–1645) of the Chosŏn Dynasty.

## Overview

Prince Sohyeon was the eldest son and crown prince of King Injo, held as a state hostage in Mukden for eight years following the Second Manchu Invasion, where he encountered Western learning and Catholicism through Jesuit missionaries.

## Features

The portal includes five main sections:

- **Home** - Introduction to Prince Sohyeon with key contextual cards
- **Chosŏn Sillok** - Interactive database of official Chosŏn historical records related to Prince Sohyeon
- **Path to Mukden** - Interactive route map showing Prince Sohyeon's journey to captivity (1637)
- **Chronological Line** - Detailed historical timeline of major events in his life and era
- **Injo Controversy** - Interactive card game exploring the succession debate following his death

## Technical Stack

- **HTML5** with semantic markup
- **CSS3** with responsive design and custom styling
- **JavaScript** for page navigation and interactivity
- **TimelineJS** for historical timeline visualization
- **Leaflet.js** for interactive mapping
- **Georgia/Garamond serif fonts** for classical academic aesthetic

## How to Use

1. Open `sohyeon-portal.html` in your web browser
2. Use the navigation menu to explore different sections
3. Each section provides different interactive experiences:
   - Database searching in Chosŏn Sillok
   - Timeline filtering by country/region
   - Interactive mapping of Sohyeon's route
   - Educational card game on succession debates

## Historical Context

Prince Sohyeon lived during a pivotal period in East Asian history:
- Second Manchu Invasion (Byeongja Horan) in 1637
- Eight years of captivity in Mukden (1637–1645)
- Exposure to Jesuit missionaries and Western learning
- Death in 1645 under controversial circumstances
- Subsequent succession crisis in the Chosŏn court
